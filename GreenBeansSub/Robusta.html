All coffee originates from a plant, believe it or not. Coffee plantations around the world expose the plant to different growing and processing conditions, which has a significant impact on the flavor. When someone uses the term "Single Origin", they are basically stating that the coffee was sourced from one location, as opposed to being some kind of blend. While there are several variables involved in the final flavor of the drink, some generalities exist based on the source of the green beans, which are basically what coffee shops and roasters recieve. It is worth noting that green beans have a very long helf life as opposed to roasted beans. It is this reason that the beans are roasted locally and sold immediately later.
