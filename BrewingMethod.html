This is information about French Press. The French press was created in France in the year 1792 and a response to King Napoleons desir for a more intense coffee experience. French press will start with coarse grind for two reasons. One being that the grounds are steeped in hot water and not washed over by drip. This enables the beans to be exctracted fully, even though they are a larger grind. The other reason is that the procese uses a mesh screen to separate the grounds from the coffee. If the beans had a regiular grind, the grounds would seep through the mesh press screen.

Expresso is made using a paticular machine which forces hot water over the grounds at high pressure. This exctracts the same mount of coffee that would end up in a cup and compresses it into a small shot.

Drip coffee is definitely the most common exptration method. A device, such as a typical coffee maker, drops boiling water over grounds contained in a filter. THis extracts the coffee and passes through a filter to keep the grounds out of the cup.
